Transcript GPA vs. LSAC GPA

Law schools see your LSAC-calculated GPA — not the GPA on your transcript. When you apply through LSAC's Credential Assembly Service (CAS), LSAC recalculates your GPA using their own standardized methodology.

A few reasons your LSAC GPA may differ from your transcript GPA:

  • LSAC includes all undergraduate coursework, even if your school excluded some grades (like repeated courses)
  • LSAC uses a standardized grading scale that may differ from your institution's own scale
  • Transfer credits and coursework from multiple institutions are all factored in
  • Graduate-level courses taken as an undergraduate may be treated differently

For most students the difference is small, but it can be meaningful. The best way to know your LSAC GPA is to register with LSAC and submit your transcript — LSAC will then calculate your official GPA from there. Your pre-law advisor can help you interpret the result.
